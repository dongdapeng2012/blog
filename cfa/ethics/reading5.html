Reading 5 The GIPS Standards

--------------------

Summary


  • The goals

    • To establish investment industry best practices for calculating and presenting investment performance that promote investor interests and instill investor confidence;

    • To obtain worldwide acceptance of a single standard for the calculation and presentation of investment performance based on the principles of fair representation and full disclosure;

    • To promote the use of accurate and consistent investment performance data;

    • To encourage fair, global competition among investment firms without creating barriers to entry;

    • To foster the notion of industry “self-regulation” on a global basis.

  • Key features

    • The GIPS standards are ethical standards for investment performance presentation to ensure fair representation and full disclosure of investment performance. In order to claim compliance, firms must adhere to the requirements included in the GIPS standards.

    • Meeting the objectives of fair representation and full disclosure is likely to require more than simply adhering to the minimum requirements of the GIPS standards. Firms should also adhere to the recommendations to achieve best practice in the calculation and presentation of performance.

    • The GIPS standards require firms to include all actual, discretionary, fee-paying portfolios in at least one composite defined by investment mandate, objective, or strategy in order to prevent firms from cherry-picking their best performance.

    • The GIPS standards rely on the integrity of input data. The accuracy of input data is critical to the accuracy of the performance presentation. The underlying valuations of portfolio holdings drive the portfolio’s performance. It is essential for these and other inputs to be accurate. The GIPS standards require firms to adhere to certain calculation methodologies and to make specific disclosures along with the firm’s performance.

    • Firms must comply with all requirements of the GIPS standards, including any updates, Guidance Statements, interpretations, Questions & Answers (Q&As), and clarifications published by CFA Institute and the GIPS Executive Committee, which are available on the GIPS website (www.gipsstandards.org) as well as in the GIPS Handbook.

  • Historical Performance Record

    • A firm is required to initially present, at a minimum, five years of annual investment performance that is compliant with the GIPS standards. If the firm or the composite has been in existence less than five years, the firm must present performance since the firm’s inception or the composite inception date.

    • After a firm presents a minimum of five years of GIPS-compliant performance (or for the period since the firm’s inception or the composite inception date if the firm or the composite has been in existence less than five years), the firm must present an additional year of performance each year, building up to a minimum of 10 years of GIPS-compliant performance.

    • Firms may link non-GIPS-compliant performance to their GIPS-compliant performance provided that only GIPS-compliant performance is presented for periods after 1 January 2000 and the firm discloses the periods of non-compliance. Firms must not link non-GIPS-compliant performance for periods beginning on or after 1 January 2000 to their GIPS-compliant performance. Firms that manage private equity, real estate, and/or wrap fee/separately managed account (SMA) portfolios must also comply with Sections 6, 7, and 8, respectively, of the Provisions of the GIPS standards that became effective as of 1 January 2006.

    1. 0. Fundamentals of Compliance

      • Fundamentals of Compliance—Requirements

        1. 0.A.1 FIRMs must comply with all the requirements of the GIPS standards, including any updates, Guidance Statements, interpretations, Questions & Answers (Q&As), and clarifications published by CFA Institute and the GIPS Executive Committee, which are available on the GIPS standards website (www.gipsstandards.org) as well as in the GIPS Handbook.

        2. 0.A.2 FIRMs must comply with all applicable laws and regulations regarding the calculation and presentation of performance.

        3. 0.A.3 FIRMs must not present performance or performance-related information that is false or misleading.

        4. 0.A.4 The GIPS standards must be applied on a FIRM-wide basis.

        5. 0.A.5 FIRMs must document their policies and procedures used in establishing and maintaining compliance with the GIPS standards, including ensuring the existence and ownership of client assets, and must apply them consistently.

        6. 0.A.6 If the FIRM does not meet all the requirements of the GIPS standards, the FIRM must not represent or state that it is “in compliance with the Global Investment Performance Standards except for . . .” or make any other statements that may indicate partial compliance with the GIPS standards.

        7. 0.A.7 Statements referring to the calculation methodology as being “in accordance,” “in compliance,” or “consistent” with the Global Investment Performance Standards, or similar statements, are prohibited.

        8. 0.A.8 Statements referring to the performance of a single, existing client portfolio as being “calculated in accordance with the Global Investment Performance Standards” are prohibited, except when a GIPS-compliant FIRM reports the performance of an individual client’s portfolio to that client.

        9. 0.A.9 FIRMs must make every reasonable effort to provide a compliant presentation to all prospective clients. FIRMs must not choose to whom they present a compliant presentation. As long as a prospective client has received a compliant presentation within the previous 12 months, the FIRM has met this requirement.

        10. 0.A.10 FIRMs must provide a complete list of composite descriptions to any prospective client that makes such a request. FIRMs must include terminated composites on the FIRM’s list of composite descriptions for at least five years after the composite termination date.

        11. 0.A.11 FIRMs must provide a compliant presentation for any composite listed on the FIRM’s list of composite descriptions to any prospective client that makes such a request.

        12. 0.A.12 FIRMs must be defined as an investment FIRM, subsidiary, or division held out to clients or prospective clients as a distinct business entity.

        13. 0.A.13 For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2011, total FIRM assets must be the aggregate fair value of all discretionary and non-discretionary assets managed by the FIRM. This includes both fee-paying and non-fee-paying portfolios.

        14. 0.A.14 Total FIRM assets must include assets assigned to a sub-advisor provided the FIRM has discretion over the selection of the sub-advisor.

        15. 0.A.15 Changes in a FIRM’s organization must not lead to alteration of historical composite performance.

        16. 0.A.16 When the FIRM jointly markets with other FIRMs, the FIRM claiming compliance with the GIPS standards must be sure that it is clearly defined and separate relative to other FIRMs being marketed, and that it is clear which FIRM is claiming compliance.

      • Fundamentals of Compliance—Recommendations

        1. 0.B.1 FIRMs should comply with the recommendations of the GIPS standards, including recommendations in any updates, Guidance Statements, interpretations, Questions & Answers (Q&As), and clarifications published by CFA Institute and the GIPS Executive Committee, which will be made available on the GIPS website (www.gipsstandards.org) as well as in the GIPS Handbook.

        2. 0.B.2 FIRMs should be verified.

        3. 0.B.3 FIRMs should adopt the broadest, most meaningful definition of the FIRM. The scope of this definition should include all geographical (country, regional, etc.) offices operating under the same brand name regardless of the actual name of the individual investment management company.

        4. 0.B.4 FIRMs should provide to each existing client, on an annual basis, a compliant presentation of the composite in which the client’s portfolio is included.

    2. 1. Input Data

      • Input Data—Requirements

        1. 1.A.1 All data and information necessary to support all items included in a compliant presentation must be captured and maintained.

        2. 1.A.2 For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2011, portfolios must be valued in accordance with the definition of fair value and the GIPS Valuation Principles.

        3. 1.A.3 Firms must value portfolios in accordance with the composite-specific valuation policy. Portfolios must be valued:

          1. For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2001, at least monthly.

          2. For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2010, on the date of all large cash flows. Firms must define large cash flow for each composite to determine when portfolios in that composite must be valued.

          3. No more frequently than required by the valuation policy.

        4. 1.A.4 For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2010, firms must value portfolios as of the calendar month end or the last business day of the month.

        5. 1.A.5 For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2005, firms must use trade date accounting.

        6. 1.A.6 Accrual accounting must be used for fixed-income securities and all other investments that earn interest income. The value of fixed-income securities must include accrued income.

        7. 1.A.7 For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2006, composites must have consistent beginning and ending annual valuation dates. Unless the composite is reported on a non-calendar fiscal year, the beginning and ending valuation dates must be at calendar year end or on the last business day of the year.

      • Input Data—Recommendations

        1. 1.B.1 Firms should value portfolios on the date of all external cash flows.

        2. 1.B.2 Valuations should be obtained from a qualified independent third party.

        3. 1.B.3 Accrual accounting should be used for dividends (as of the ex-dividend date).

        4. 1.B.4 Firms should accrue investment management fees.

    3. 2. Calculation Methodology

      • Calculation Methodology—Requirements

        1. 2.A.1 Total returns must be used.

        2. 2.A.2 Firms must calculate time-weighted rates of return that adjust for external cash flows. Both periodic and sub-period returns must be geometrically linked. External cash flows must be treated according to the firm’s composite-specific policy. At a minimum:

          1. For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2001, firms must calculate portfolio returns at least monthly.

          2. For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2005, firms must calculate portfolio returns that adjust for daily-weighted external cash flows.

        3. 2.A.3 Returns from cash and cash equivalents held in portfolios must be included in all return calculations.

        4. 2.A.4 All returns must be calculated after the deduction of the actual trading expenses incurred during the period. Firms must not use estimated trading expenses.

        5. 2.A.5 If the actual trading expenses cannot be identified and segregated from a bundled fee:

          1. When calculating gross-of-fees returns, returns must be reduced by the entire bundled fee or the portion of the bundled fee that includes the trading expenses. Firms must not use estimated trading expenses.

          2. When calculating net-of-fees returns, returns must be reduced by the entire bundled fee or the portion of the bundled fee that includes the trading expenses and the investment management fee. Firms must not use estimated trading expenses.

        6. 2.A.6 Composite returns must be calculated by asset-weighting the individual portfolio returns using beginning-of-period values or a method that reflects both beginning-of-period values and external cash flows.

        7. 2.A.7 Composite returns must be calculated:

          1. For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2006, by asset-weighting the individual portfolio returns at least quarterly.

          2. For periods beginning on or after 1 January 2010, by asset-weighting the individual portfolio returns at least monthly.

      • Calculation Methodology—Recommendations

        1. 2.B.1 Returns should be calculated net of non-reclaimable withholding taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains. Reclaimable withholding taxes should be accrued.

        2. 2.B.2 For periods prior to 1 January 2010, firms should calculate composite returns by asset-weighting the individual portfolio returns at least monthly.

中文手册整理


  • CFA协会-全球投资业绩标准 Global Investment Performance Standards，GIPS

  • 起因？

    • 过去的衡量方法阻碍了公司之间的业绩比较

      • 代表性账户

      • 存活者偏差

      • 选取时间区间

    • GIPS标准化全行业通用的准则，避免业绩误导性陈述

  • 宣称遵守GIPS的规定

    • 遵守GIPS是自愿的

    • 任何实际管理资产的投资公司都可以遵守

    • 遵守GIPS必须是全公司的（firm-wide）

    • [firm name] has prepared and presented this report in compliance with the Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS)

  • 获益者？

    • 投资管理公司与投资者

      • 获得和世界各地的合规公司竞争的机会

      • 增强公司在业绩方面的内控制度

      • 投资者信任遵守GIPS的公司的业绩披露

      • 投资者能在不同的投资公司之间比较业绩

  • 组合群（composites）

    • 由投资组合（portfolio）构成，代表特定投资目标或投资策略的集合

    • 一定要包含全部的付费的（fee paying）全权委托的（discretionary）资产组合，包括当前和以前的投资组合

    • 事先安排（ex-ante basis）包括哪些投资组合，防止投资公司只选择表现好的投资组合

    • 非全权委托的（nondiscretionary）资产组合一定不能包括在组合群中

  • 组合群 vs 资产组合

    • 资产组合是基于现代投资组合理论建立的各种资产的集合，主要考虑风险与回报的关系

    • 组合群除了考虑风险与回报的关系，还考虑既定策略或目标的实现

    • 投资组合从投资者角度出发，与个人的风险偏好有很大关系

    • 组合群是投资公司的选择，体现投资公司的某个特定投资策略或投资目标

    • 一个投资公司不会只有一个投资组合，但可以只有一个组合群

    • 组合群收益率用组合群中所有投资组合的资产加权收益率（asset weighted rate of return）表示

    • 相同投资目标或投资策略的投资组合必须纳入一个组合群中，避免投资公司只选择表现好的投资组合

    • 组合群与综合指数（composite index）的表现相似，组合群的市场价值经常变动，且不易计算

  • 公司构建组合群时必须满足

    • 所有付费的全权委托的资产组合必须至少包含在一个组合群中

    • 资产组合群必须按照相似的投资目标或投资策略进行定义和界定

    • 公司在管理新的投资组合时，组合群必须及时更新

    • 已终止的投资组合必须包含在适当的组合群的历史业绩记录中

    • 除非有客户的书面变更指示或对组合群重新界定，否则投资组合不得在组合群中转换

    • 可转债和其他混合型债券的处理在不同时期必须保持一致

    • 不包括现金的分离资产（carve-out segments，客户自行管理），不是全权委托的投资组合

    • 组合群只能包括公司实际管理的资产，不包括模拟的投资组合

    • 如果公司设定了进入组合群的投资组合的最小资产，那么所有低于这个资产值的投资组合都不能进入这个组合群

  • 认证

    • GIPS是独立的第三方机构对投资管理公司的绩效评价过程和程序进行检查与核实

    • 公司不能自我认证

    • 认证在全公司范围下进行，不能保证特定组合群业绩展示的准确性

    • 认证主要考察

      • 是否在全公司层面（firm-wide basis）遵守GIPS对组合群构建的要求和条件

      • 公司指定的业绩计算呈报机制及程序是否符合GIPS规定

  • 目标

    • 建立全球认可的，公允的（fair），可比较的，有全面披露的投资表现计算和陈述标准

    • 确保呈报，记录，营销，陈述时均使用准确一致的投资表现数据

    • 促使市场上所有投资管理公司进行公平的全球化的竞争，同时不建立准入壁垒

    • 在全球范围内推行业内自我监管理念

  • 基本要求

    • 实际管理资产的独立商业实体才能宣传遵守GIPS

    • GIPS是为陈述业绩而设的道德标准，确保公正陈述业绩并全面披露

    • GIPS规定公司必须将所有实际付费并全权管理的投资组合纳入组合群

    • GIPS要求公司提供至少5年的合规历史，并每年增加，直至至少十年历史

    • 公司组织结构的变化不能更改组合群的历史业绩

    • GIPS规定公司采用一定的计算及报告方法，并披露

    • GIPS规定只能用真实的数据

    • GIPS包括声称合规的公司所必须遵守的以及鼓励遵守的条文

    • GIPS必须全公司实施，公司必须是独立的商业实体

    • 如果公司有自主选择二级投资顾问（sub-advisor）的权力，则必须在组合群中纳入这部分资产的业绩

    • 如果法律与GIPS冲突，公司必须完全披露

    • 目前GIPS推荐的项目，未来可能必须采用

    • GIPS包括私募股权及房地产的补充条款

  • GIPS9个组成部分

    • 0.合规基本要求

      • 公司的定义-要求条款

        • GIPS必须全公司实施；公司必须是独立的商业实体

        • 如果公司有自主选择二级投资顾问（sub-advisor）的权力，则必须在组合群中纳入这部分资产的业绩

        • 公司组织结构的变化不能更改组合群的历史业绩

      • 公司的定义-推荐条款

        • 公司的定义应该包括同一品牌下所有区域办事处

      • 文档化政策及程序-要求条款

        • 公司必须以书面形式制定其遵循GIPS所有要求条款的政策和程序

      • 合规声明-要求条款

        • 完全遵守或完全不遵守

        • 组合群业绩报告没有‘与GIPS相符’‘与GIPS计算要求一致’一说

      • 公司基本责任-要求条款

        • 公司必须向所有潜在客户提供符合GIPS标准的报告（必须在之前的12个月内）

        • 公司至少将已终止的组合群保留5年

        • 潜在客户可以要求公司提供组合群清单和描述

        • 客户可以要求公司提供符合GIPS标准的报告，组合群清单和描述

        • 当公司与其他公司一起参与营销时，宣称遵守GIPS的公司必须确保资产是明确定义的，独立于非GIPS合规公司

    • 1.数据输入

      • 数据输入的连贯性对GIPS极为重要，是建立符合GIPS标准业绩报告的基础

      • 从2011年1月1日起，资产组合必须按照公允价值计算

    • 2.计算方法

      • 统一的收益计算方法来计算资产组合（时间加权），组合群（资产加权）的收益率

      • 一旦选定计算方法就应该持续下去，以方便后期比较

    • 3.组合群的构建

    • 4.信息披露

      • 信息披露对业绩报告的原始数据进行详细说明，使最终用户理解业绩结果

      • GIPS要求公司披露业绩报告及其采纳的政策

      • 有些披露适用于所有公司，有些适用于特定公司

    • 5.陈述与呈报

      • 5年，逐年增加，直至10年

      • 在输入数据计算收益构建组合群并确定披露信息之后，公司必须按照条款的规定，报告和披露信息

      • 如有必要，公司应该适时地将GIPS没有涉及的信息披露纳入业绩报告，比如组合群收益应注明是总费用还是净费用

    • 6.房地产

      • 房地产包括土地建筑等，某些条款适用于所有房地产投资，不论公司的实际控制权，是否创造收益或者有无杠杆

    • 7.私募股权投资

      • 必须按照GIPS关于PE估值的规定进行估值

      • 开放式私募（private equity open-end fund）和长青基金（evergreen fund）不遵守‘7.私募股权投资’而是遵守0-5部分要求

      • 封闭式房地产基金（real estate closed-end fund）必须遵守‘6.房地产’的要求

      • 私募股权投资包括所有非公开交易的对公司的投资形式，不论处在哪一阶段，所以包括风险投资，上市公司私有化投资，夹层融资（mezzanine financing），有限合伙人投资，基金的基金投资

    • 8.打包费用/专户管理资产组合

      • 打包费用（wrap fee）指一种费用打包形式，适用于特定投资产品

      • 打包费用投资组合有时被称为‘专户理财’SMA

      • 上述0-5部分的一些要求条款和推荐条款仅作为补充性条款，或者直接被‘8.打包费用/专户管理资产组合’的要求条款取代
