STANDARD III: DUTIES TO CLIENTS

--------------------

Standard III(A) Loyalty, Prudence, and Care

适用情景

  • 确认真正的客户是谁
  • Client Profile
  • Soft Commission Policies
  • Proxy Voting Policies

    Recommended Procedures for Compliance
  • Regular Account Information
    • 向每一位客户至少季报, 账户和交易信息
    • 告知客户资产在何时何处移动
    • 客户资产完全独立

  • Client Approval
    • 有问题 --> 换位思考客户需要什么 --> 书面询问

  • Firm Policies
    • Follow all applicable rules and laws
    • Investment objectives of the client
    • Consider all the information when taking actions
    • Diversify
    • Regular review
    • Deal fairly
    • Disclose conflicts of interest
    • Disclose compensation
    • Vote shares or proxies in the best interests of the clients and ultimate beneficiaries
    • Maintain confidentiality
    • Seek best execution
    • Place client interests first

Examples

Example 1 (Identifying the Client—Plan Participants)

Although Andy, work in a trustee bank for a pension plan, believes the stock is overvalued, he purchases the stock to maintain MMM's good favor toward the trustee bank.


Example 2 (Client Commission Practices)

TTT's commission structure is higher than other firms. In exchange for JJJ directing its brokerage to TTT, TTT absorbs a number of JJJ overhead expenses, including those for rent.


Example 3 (Brokerage Arrangements)

SSS Company gives Andy the best price and practice. To induce SSS to send more new accounts to him, Andy directs transactions to SSS from other clients without their knowledge.

Comment : best price and execution and the practice is disclosed to the accounts

Example 4-7 omit

Example 8 (Identifying the Client)

Andy is hired by FFF, whose work regulated by local law. Andy thinks some work may have negative consequences for FFF.

Comment : "who is the client" is not well defined. independence and objectivity. not let the law firm influence the legal proceedings.


Example 9 (Identifying the Client)

Andy has a new client CCC company, who is one investor in a mutual fund managed by Bob. Andy states that the two of them now share a responsibility to this client.

Comment : Andy's client is CCC. Bob's client is all investors of the fund, including CCC.

Example 10 omit

Example 11 (Execution-Only Responsibilities)

Andy is a broker who executes client-directed trades for several individuals. Andy does not provide any investment advice and only executes the trading decisions made by clients.

Comment : Andy serves in an execution-only capacity and his duty of loyalty, prudence, and care is centered on the skill and diligence used when executing trades, by seeking best execution and making trades directed by the clients. His duty is that trades are executed on the most favorable terms. No requirement of giving advice.

Standard III(B) Fair Dealing

Highlights

Standard III(B) does not state "equally" because members and candidates could not possibly reach all clients at exactly the same time

not all investment opportunities are suitable for all clients

M&C may differentiate their services to clients, but must not disadvantage or negatively affect clients, and should be disclosed and available to clients and prospective clients

Examples

Example 1 (Selective Disclosure)

While the report is being reviewed by the company, Andy schedules a luncheon with several of his best clients to discuss the company. At the luncheon, he mentions the purchase recommendation scheduled to be sent early the following week to all the firm's clients.

Example 2-4 omit

Example 5 (Selective Disclosure)

Andy repeats the US$1.16 figure in his research report to all Coffey clients but informally tells his large clients that he expects the earnings per share to be higher.

Comment : By not sharing his opinion regarding the potential for a significant upside earnings surprise with all clients, Andy is not treating all clients fairly and has violated Standard III(B).


Example 6 (Additional Services for Select Clients)

Andy uses e-mail to issue a new recommendation to all his clients. He then calls his three largest institutional clients to discuss the recommendation in detail.

Comment : not violated


Example 7 (Minimum Lot Allocations)

Andy determines that a new bond is appropriate for five of her clients. Three request US$10,000 each, and the other two request US$50,000 each. The minimum lot size is US$5,000 to not affect the liquidity at the time of sale. Andy will receive only US$55,000 for all accounts. He distributes the bond investments as follows: The three accounts that requested US$10,000 are allocated US$5,000 each, and the two accounts that requested US$50,000 are allocated US$20,000 each.

Comment : Andy ensured that each client received at least the minimum lot size.

Example 8 omit

Example 9 (Limited Social Media Disclosures)

In her excitement, Andy sends to FFF Twitter followers summarizing her "buy" recommendation for SSS stock.

Comment : violated Standard III(B) by sending an investment recommendation to a select group of contacts prior to distributing it to all clients

Example 10 omit

Standard III(C) Suitability

Highlights

适用情景

  • 投资策略
  • 客户的风险概况
  • 分散化需求
  • 客户的交易指令
  • 产品是否适合客户

    Recommended Procedures for Compliance
  • Investment Policy Statement
    • client identification
      • type and nature of client
      • beneficiaries
      • approximate portion of total client assets that the M&C is managing
    • investor objectives
      • return objectives
      • risk tolerance
    • investor constraints
      • liquidity
      • expected cash flows
      • investable funds
      • time horizon
      • tax
      • regulatory and legal circumstances
      • investor preferences, prohibitions, circumstances, and unique needs
      • proxy voting responsibilities and guidance
    • benchmarks

  • Suitability Test Policies
    • potential return in long period
    • impact on the portfolio's diversification
    • investment risks vs. risk tolerance
    • fit of investment with the investment strategy

Examples

Example 1 omit

Example 2 (Investment Suitability—Entire Portfolio)

Andy, an investment adviser, suggests to Bob, a risk-averse client, that covered call options be used in his equity portfolio. Andy educates Bob about all possible outcomes.

Comment : Andy properly considers the investment in the context of the entire portfolio and thoroughly explains the investment to the client.


Example 3 (IPS Updating)

In a regular meeting with client Bob, the portfolio managers review his current needs and circumstances.


Example 4 (Following an Investment Mandate)

Andy manages a high-income mutual fund. He purchases zero-dividend stock.


Example 5 (IPS Requirements and Limitations)

Andy wants to improve the diversification and increase returns. The investment policy statement requires high liquidity. A venture capital group offered very attractive prospective returns on funds that provide seed capital to ventures.

Example 6-7 omit

Example 8 (Investment Suitability)

The new CCC fund consists of two top-performing hedge funds managed. Andy believes this new fund is a winning combination for all parties.

Comment : The new fund may not be suitable for every client. As an investment adviser, Andy needs to establish an investment policy statement for each client and recommend only investments that match each client's risk and return profile in the IPS.

Standard III(D) Performance Presentation

Highlights

provide credible performance information

avoid misstating performance or misleading performance

    Recommended Procedures for Compliance
  • Apply the GIPS Standards

  • Compliance without Applying GIPS Standards
    • considering the knowledge and sophistication of the audience
    • presenting the performance of portfolios, not a single representative account
    • including terminated accounts in performance history with clarify terminated time
    • including fully disclosures what performance results it is
    • maintaining the data and records as source of the performance

Examples

Example 1 omit

Example 2 (Performance Calculation and Asset Weighting)

The firm claims compliance with the GIPS standards, but returns are not in accordance with the GIPS standards.


Example 3 (Performance Presentation and Prior Fund/Employer)

In developing MMM's advertising and marketing campaign, Andy prepares an advertisement that includes the equity investment performance he achieved at GGG Financial.

Comment : This standard does not prohibit showing past performance at a prior firm as long as disclosures where the performance took place and the person's specific role.

Standard III(E) Preservation of Confidentiality

Highlights

适用情景

  • 不论客户/潜在客户/前客户
  • 电子信息安全
  • 合法
  • PCP调查

    Recommended Procedures for Compliance
  • Before making a disclosure, M&C should ask
    • In what context was the information disclosed? If disclosed in a discussion of work being performed for the client, is the information relevant to the work?
    • Is the information help the M&C to improve service to the client?

Examples

Example 1 omit

Example 2 (Disclosing Confidential Information)

An advisory customer talked to Andy about giving approximately US$ 1M to charity to reduce his income taxes. Andy recommends his client GGG for corporate gifts call on his customer and ask for a donation.

Comment : Even though the attempt to help GGG and the customer was well intended, Andy violated Standard III(E) by revealing confidential information about his client.


Example 3 (Disclosing Possible Illegal Activity)

Government officials approach Andy, to examine pension fund records of GGG Company. Andy consults his employer's general counsel and is advised that GGG has probably violated tax and fiduciary regulations and laws.

Comment : Andy should inform his supervisor of these activities, and his employer should take steps with GGG to remedy the violations. Andy and his employer should seek advice of legal counsel for further actions. Andy may have a duty to disclose the evidence of the continuing legal violations and to resign as asset manager for GGG.


Example 4 (Disclosing Possible Illegal Activity)

Based on the financial records, Andy believes that the CFO is embezzling money from the corporation into his personal investment account.

Comment : Andy should check with his firm's compliance department or appropriate legal counsel to determine whether applicable regulations require reporting the CFO's financial records.


Example 5 (Accidental Disclosure of Confidential Information)

One of Andy's clients responded directly on the group page that his family recently experienced a major change, with highly personal and confidential details of the event. Unfortunately, all clients were also able to read the detailed posting until Andy was able to have the comment removed.

Comment : The accidental disclosure of confidential information by a client is not under Andy's control. His actions to remove the information promptly align with Standard III(E).
