Standard I(A) Knowledge of the Law

Highlights

EE must understand the applicable laws and regulations

EE should know ER's policies and procedures for accessing compliance guidance.

During regulations changing,

  • EE must remain vigilant in maintaining their knowledge of the requirements
  • EE and ER adjust their procedures and practices to remain in complianc

适用情景

  • 参与违规 Known Violations And Involved: 上报 --> 拒绝 --> 离开
  • 知道他人违规 Known Violations But Not Involved: 上报 --> 撇清 --> 咨询
  • 不确定自己或他人是否违规 Not sure: 咨询独立法律意见
  • Investment Products and Applicable Laws 居住地/业务地/C&S, 适用法律

Examples

Example 1 (Notification of Known Violations)

An official indicates Andy that the financial statement Andy filed with the regulator overstate the issuer's earnings. Andy seeks the advice of the ER's general counsel, who states that it would be difficult for the regulator to prove that Andy has been involved in any wrongdoing.

Comment : 独立法律意见


Example 2 (Dissociating form a Violation)

Andy's ER is the principal underwriter for an issue of convertible debentures by the CCC Company. Andy discovers that the CCC Company has concealed severe losses in its foreign operations . The preliminary prospectus has already been distributed.

Comment : Known Violations But Not Involved: 上报 --> 撇清 --> 咨询

  • Report to the appropriate supervisory persons in his ER
  • If no change, Andy should sever all his connections with the underwriting
  • Seek legal advice


Example 3 (Dissociating form a Violation)

Andy's ER advertises its X-year return of a composite of its client accounts. Andy discovers that the composite omits the accounts that have left the ER during the period, whereas the description indicates the inclusion of all ER accounts. This omission has led to an inflated performance figure. Andy is asked to use the erroneous performance number when soliciting business .

Comment : Known Violations And Involved: 上报 --> 拒绝 --> 离开

  • report to his supervisor or the compliance department at his ER.
  • If no change, he must refrain from using the misleading material.
  • If the ER insists, he should consider whether to seek other employment.


Example 4 (Following the Highest Requirements)

Andy is an investment analyst for a Wall Street brokerage ER. He works in a developing country with a rapidly modernizing economy and a growing capital market. Local securities laws includes no punitive prohibitions against insider trading.

Comment : 居住地/业务地适用的法律 vs. C&S


Example 5 (Following the Highest Requirements)

Andy works for a multinational investment adviser based in the United States. Andy lives and works as in another countryX. Andy believing that he should comply only with US law. And as long as he adheres to the Code and Standards requirement that he disclose his participation in any IPO to his ER and clients is enough.

Comment : 居住地/业务地适用的法律 vs. C&S


Example 6 (Laws and Regulations Based on Religious Tenets)

Andy's ER receives expressions of interest from potential clients who are seeking investments that comply with Islamic law. The marketing and promotional materials for the fund do not specify whether or not the fund is compliance with Islamic law.

Example 7 omit

Example 8 (Failure to Maintain Knowledge of the Law)

Andy recently began posting investment information to his Facebook page with misleading words. Prior to Andy's use of these social media platforms, the local regulator had issued new requirements and guidance governing online electronic communication. Andy's communications appear to conflict with the recent regulatory announcements.

Comment : Andy should seek guidance from appropriate, knowledgeable, and reliable sources, such as his ER's compliance department, external service providers, or outside counsel.

Standard I(B) Independence and Objectivity

Highlights

works and opinions unaffected by any potential conflict of interest or other circumstance adversely affecting their judgment.

适用情景

    只要不影响独立客观性, 且不被认为很可能会影响独立客观性
  • 来自利益相关方的压力和诱惑
  • 自身绩效/制度的压力和诱惑
  • Public Companies
  • Credit Rating Angency Opinions
  • 雇佣, 招投标
  • 付费的研究
  • 差旅费
  • 礼品与奖励

    Recommended Procedures for Compliance
  • Protect the intefrity of opinions
    • EE & ER, establish policies
    • ER, design compensation systems

  • Create a restricted list

  • Restrict special cost arrangements
    • EE and ER
      • should pay for commercial transportation and hotel charges.
      • should encourage issuers to limit the use of corporate aircraft to situations in which commercial transportation hard to be arranged
    • Client
      • should not reimburse members or candidates for air transportation
      • should not always host the M&C

  • Limit gifts
    • EE : as long as its purpose is not to influence or reward members or candidates
    • ER : a strict value limit

  • Restrict investments
    • EE encourage firms to develop formal policies to EE purchases of equity or IPOs
    • ER prior approval for EE participation in IPOs, with prompt disclosure of investment actions
    • Strict limits in private placements

  • Review procedures
    • EE should encourage their firms to implement effective supervisory and review procedures to ensure that analysts and portfolio managers comply with policies relating to their personal investment activities

  • Independence policy
    • formal written policy on the independence and objectivity of research and implement reporting structures
    • review procedures to ensure it

  • Appointed officer
    • appoint a senior officer
    • ER should provide every EE with the procedures and policies

Examples

Example 1 (Travel Expenses)

Andy accepts CCC arranges for flights and accommodations in SSS Motels. as do the other analysts, with one exception John Adams from a large trust company who insists on following his company's policy and paying himself.

Comment : as long as no reason Andy's independence and objectivity were compromised


Example 2 (Research Independence)

Andy is making a presentation to a potential new business client that includes the promise of a full research coverage of the potential client.

Comment : must not commit a favorable recommendation.


Example 3 (Research Independence and Intrafirm Pressure)

Andy has concluded that the stock of MMM is overpriced, but he is concerned that a negative research report will hurt the good relationship between MMM and the investment banking division of his ER.

Comment : restricted list

Example 7 (Gifts and Entertainment from Related Party

Andy directs a large amount of his commission business to a brokerage house. The brokerage house gives Andy two tickets to the World Cup in South Africa, with a nearby resort, meals, and local transportation. Andy fails to disclose receiving this package to his supervisor.

Comment : may impede independence and objectivity


Example 8 (Gifts and Entertainment from Client)

Andy achieves an annual better return than agreed to. As a reward, Jackson offers Andy two tickets and the use of Jackson's flat in London for a week. Andy discloses this gift to his supervisor.

Comment : Disclosure is required.


Example 9 (Travel Expenses from External Manager)

Andy followed the plan's standard procedure of seeking presentations from a number of qualified firms and recommended that his board select PPP because of its experience, well-defined investment strategy, and performance record. PPP was one of the sponsors of an trip to Asia that Andy made earlier in the year. The Pension Investment Academy obtains support for the cost, including PPP Advisors, and pays the travel expenses. The president of PPP was on the trip.

Comment : should be paid by his ER or the pension plan. PPP also has more opportunity to communicate with Andy, which is an advantage than other competitors.


Example 10 (Research Independence and Compensation Arrangements)

Andy was hired to write a research report on a client. ER will pay Andy a flat fee plus a bonus if any new investors as a result of Andy's report.

Comment : the bonus linked to attract clients is expected to compromise his independence and objectivity


Example 11 (Recommendation Objectivity and Service Fees)

Two years ago, WWW Funds offered Andy to pay a service fee of 0.2%. Without disclosing the fee, Andy asked one investment manager to review the funds. The manager completed the normal due diligence review and determined that the new funds were fairly valued. Andy decided to accept WWW's offer. Now the funds begin to underperform. Andy continues to recommend these funds.

Comment : Fee impede objectivity. Standard VI(A)–Disclosure of Conflicts.


Example 12 (Recommendation Objectivity)

Andy hopes that an analysis should include a scenario run with –10% for Year 1 and 2, and then 0% for Years 3 through 5. The manager replies that these assumptions are too dire because there has never been a time in their available database when the index was negative . Andy conducts his research in the worst-case scenario, and based on the results Andy does not recommend the purchase of the fund.

Comment : should run the worst-case scenario


Example 13 (Influencing Manager Selection Decisions)

To improve CCC's chances of winning, Andy made significant monetary contributions to Gomez's union reelection campaign fund. All of Andy's outlays were routinely handled as marketing expenses reimbursed by CCC and were disclosed to his senior management.

Comment : reasonably be expected to compromise Gomez's objectivity


Example 14 (Influencing Manager Selection Decisions)

Andy decided to improve CCC's chances of being invited to participate in the search competition by befriending Finlay to curry his favor. Knowing Finlay's love of entertainment, Andy wined and dined Finlay at high-profile bistros where Finlay could glow in the fan recognition lavished on him by all the other patrons. Andy's endeavors paid off handsomely when Finlay recommended CCC.


Example 15 (Fund Manager Relationships)

Andy notices a reported composite construction concealing the bad performance of a particularly large account by placing that account into a new composite.

Comment : Andy should disclose the change in his report


Example 16 (Intrafirm Pressure)

One larger account holder to experience significant underperformance because of the poor quality of their recommendations, and withdraw his money. The head of sales requests to remove this account from performance composite.

Standard I(C) Misrepresentation

Highlights

A misrepresentation is any untrue / false / misleading statement or omission of a fact.

M&C should not guarantee clients any specific return on volatile investments.

M&C should not attribution or knowingly use plagiarized materials.

适用情景

  • Impact on investment practice
  • 业绩表述 真实, 澄清税前税后, 是真实账户还是模拟, 账户选取
  • 社交媒体 信息及时, 与公司文件一致
  • 遗漏信息
  • 抄袭
  • 归雇主所有的成果

Examples

Example 1 (Disclosure of Issuer-Paid Research)

Andy is an issuer-paid analyst. Andy creates a website that promotes his research efforts as a seemingly independent analyst and posts a profile and a strong buy recommendation for each company on the website. He does not disclose the contractual relationships with the companies he covers on his website.


Example 2 (Correction of Unintentional Errors)

Andy creates and distributes a presentation of performance states the composite has ¥350 billion in assets, but should be only ¥35 billion actually, due to a typographical error.

Comment : Once the error is discovered, Andy must take steps to cease distribution and inform those who have received the erroneous information.


Example 3 (Noncorrection of Known Errors)

ER's promotional material claims that Andy has an advanced degree in finance from a prestigious business school. Andy attended the school for a short period of time but did not receive a degree. Over the years, Andy and others in the firm have distributed this material to numerous prospective clients and consultants.

Comment : Andy used this material numerous times over an extended period and should have known of the misrepresentation.


Example 4 (Plagiarism)

Andy recently read an extensive research report by Bob, providing extensive statistics but Bob cited no specific data as to the initial drilling results. Andy called an officer of Jefferson, who gave the initial data. The data indicated that the expected life of the main mines would be tripled. Andy added these statistics to the report and circulated it within his firm as his own report.

Comment : Andy plagiarized Bob's report by reproducing large parts of it in his own report without acknowledgment.


Example 5 (Misrepresentation of Information)

When Andy sells mortgage-backed derivatives calle "interest-only strips" (IOs) to public pension plan clients, he describes them as "guaranteed by the US government".


Example 6 (Potential Information Misrepresentation)

Andy manages the investments in the United States for clients are approaching retirement. Andy advises these individuals that a portion of their investments be moved from equity to bank-sponsored certificates of deposit and money market accounts so that the principal will be "guaranteed" up to a certain amount.

Comment : Although there is risk that the institution could go bankrupt, in the United States, these accounts are insured by the US government through the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation.


Example 7 (Plagiarism)

Andy reports his conclusions to the partner in charge, who tells him to "use the Davis report, change a few words, sign your name."


Example 8 (Plagiarism)

Andy returns from a seminar where Jack discussed his new models in great detail. Andy makes some minor mechanical changes but retains the concepts, and quickly announces to his supervisors that he has discovered a new model and that clients and prospective clients should be informed of this positive finding.

Comment : Andy can take credit for the final, practical results; he can also support his conclusions with his own test. The credit for the innovative thinking must be awarded to Jack.


Example 9 (Plagiarism)

Andy would like to include in his firm's marketing materials some "plainlanguage" descriptions of various general concepts. The descriptions come from other sources, but Andy wishes to use them without reference to the original authors.

Comment : Copying verbatim any material without acknowledgement, including plain-language descriptions of general concepts, violates Standard I(C). Best practice would be to describe them in his own words or cite the sources. M&C should not attribution or knowingly use plagiarized materials.


Example 10 (Plagiarism)

Through a mainstream media, Andy learns about a study that Andy would like to cite in his research.

Comment : In all instances, M&C must cite the actual source. Best practice for Schneider would be to obtain the information directly from the author and review it before citing it in a report. In this case, best practice would be either to

  • obtain the complete study from its original author and cite only that author
    • or
  • to use the information provided by the intermediary and cite both sources


Example 11 (Misrepresentation of Information)

Andy's firm subscribes to a service from a investment research firm that provides reports that can be repackaged by smaller firms for those firms' clients. Andy's firm distributes these reports to clients as its own work.


Example 12 (Misrepresentation of Information)

Andy becomes aware that AAA is promoting with some issue. Supervisor says that AAA owns the product and is responsible for all marketing and distribution, and the product is outside of the US regulatory regime that ER follows and that all risks of the product are disclosed at page 133 of the prospectus.

Comment : Andy should continue to pursue the issue of AAA's inaccurate promotion of the portfolio according to the firm's policies and procedures.


Example 13 (Avoiding a Misrepresentation)

Because of the complexity of highly structured mortgage securities, Andy's team cannot effectively distinguish between potentially good and bad investment options. The team decides that not the highly structured mortgage segment of the securitized market but the less complex securities could become part of the core of the fund's portfolio.

Comment : Andy is in compliance with Standard I(C) by not investing in securities that he and the team cannot effectively understand.


Example 14 (Misrepresenting Composite Construction)

The fund manager is quite liberal in composite construction. The reason accounts are included/excluded is not fully explained.

Comment : Cherry picking


Example 15 (Presenting Out-of-Date Information)

Andy knows that some of the fact sheets are out of date. He continues to provide the sheets to the sales team without updates because the bank has no control over the marketing material.


Example 16 (Overemphasis of Firm Results)

Andy's firm currently offering eight funds to clients. Seven of the eight had X-year returns below the median for their respective sectors. Andy approves a recent advertisement stating: "Optima Asset Management is achieving excellent returns for its investors. The FFFF fund, for example, has exceed the sector median by more than 10%."

Comment : may be attempting to mislead potential clients by citing the only positive fund but conceal the negative ones.

Standard I(D) Misconduct

Highlights

Any act that involves lying, cheating, stealing, or other dishonest conduct is a violation of this standard

Examples

Example 1 (Professionalism and Competence)

Andy's clients have observedclients have observed him having numerous drinks. Back at work after lunch, he clearly is intoxicated while making investment decisions.


Example 2 (Fraud and Deceit)

Andy submits reimbursement forms over a two-year period to AAA's self-funded health insurance program for more than two dozen bills, most of which have been altered to increase the amount due.


Example 3 (Fraud and Deceit)

Andy agrees to a price 20% higher than normal and splits the surcharge with his friend .


Example 4 (Personal Actions and Integrity)

Andy has been arrested on numerous occasions for trespassing on a large petrochemical plant that is accused of damaging the environment.

Comment : only lying, cheating, stealing, or other dishonest conduct is a violation


Example 5 (Professional Misconduct)

Andy observes that the reported performance of a hedge fund does not reflect the fact. He approaches the head of trading about his concern and is told that he should not ask any questions. He contacts the compliance officer, who also tells his to stay away from the issue.

Comment : Should all internal communications within the firm not satisfy his concerns, Andy should consider reporting to the appropriate regulator.
